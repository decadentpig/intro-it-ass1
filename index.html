Personal Info

My name is Jordan De Visser, I’m a student and public servant living in Brisbane. I am Australian with Swedish heritage, and I speak English (plus a vanishing amount of Hindi). In my spare time I write short fiction. Recently, I finished editing the first issue of a Brisbane-based literary magazine called Urinal Mag, which is available in print soon. It collects short stories and poetry from around my city.

Interest in IT

I have been interested in technology my entire life. My first vivid memory of a computer involves playing Lemmings on a tobacco-beige Windows 95 machine. Since then I have always kept up to date with developments in hardware and software.

Most of my IT experience is informal. I work in administration, and so part of my job inevitably involves some technical support (for printers, fax machines, enterprise software etc.). This support work is usually improvised and definitely additional to my job description. During COVID I began learning HTML, CSS and JavaScript, and find the web development space vibrant and interesting.

I chose RMIT for a variety of reasons. I have known people who have been RMIT students in the past, and I’ve also been impressed by RMIT's rankings in the country and worldwide. During my studies I expect to gain both a macro and a micro view of computers and the tech industry. I expect to learn a lot about the variety of specialisations within the industry, and to gain specific knowledge and skills for getting involved.
